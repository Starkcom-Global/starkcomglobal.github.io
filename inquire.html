Connecting the World

As mentioned when you have most of the world relying on outdated infrastructure, you get an overloaded grid and an extremely unreliable internet. Added costs also include an ever growing space debris problem, and an increasing reliance on rocket fuel for launches. Not to mention that for the LEO satellites that do burn in earth's orbit, they have a very low shelf life and a huge sustainability problem. 

Outside of these problems, and a huge reliance on cabling and towers, are the third of the world that can't afford legacy tech or are unconnected. As our home page states, we are connecting the unconnected.

Transportation / Launches

From Tethered to LEO

We can do launches that are tethered (5000ft), stratospheric, Kármán line, and LEO depending on regulations, flyers, launch needs, and budget. 

Most launches on a commercial or low test use setting are set to be tethered or stratospheric launches, especially if it is within connecting 10k to 100k people. High budget launches and population dense areas are different. 

Solutions/ Sensing

Real-time Monitoring and Sensing

We are working on real-time data monitoring solutions from being able to track our satellites to being able to track our launch reservations and even connectivity or data transmission events. Being oriented towards real-time data monitoring allows us to build a variety of telemetry solutions to meet the connectivity needs of our customers.

Included in these needs have been the need to monitor various issues such as underwater wireless networks and telemetry, oceanic carbon capturing, our CubeSats and gateways, as well as custom solutions centered around IoT and smart city usecases.

From CubeSat to Gateway

Our CubeSat to Gateway model is built where our CubeSats are developed to transmit signal data to our gateways. This will allow for our gateways to pick up reception and for connected devices to be able to access the internet at relatively high speeds.
