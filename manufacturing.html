How we do business?

Built for horizontal scaling

From contacting us, to setting up coordinates and figuring out a scope of work, to actually getting an agreement signed, funds in escrow, and a launch initiated, we want to be here with you every step in the way.

Initial Contact and Inquiry

/ 01

Once you submit the form, the Starkcom Global team (Stark Drones) can get back to you with some initial information.

Gathering Client Information

/ 02

Once we gather the client information, we can agree on a scope of work, and you start getting inputted into our system.

Agreement is Initiated

/ 03

After understanding all the client's concerns and the scope of work has been agreed upon, a contract is signed and funds are in escrow.

Launch is Scheduled

/ 04

The manufacturing process has begun, and we are ready to start building your devices and preparing for the production and launch of your CubeSats.

IoT Compatible

We are IoT compatible and that includes Arduino, Raspberry PI, Particle IO, and software like ChirpStack.

Embedded Gateways

We have embedded gateways which is the Stark Compute module and it aims to be horizontally scaled as time goes on.

Realtime Data

We are working on dashboards for collecting real-time data and for numerous real-time data sensing capabilities. 

Tracking Launches

Stark Maps and other potential software solutions have been in the works as targets for launch tracking and mapping connectivity.

Manufacturing

We are working on redesigning each variation of our launch vehicles and methods from the ground up from generation to generation. Our first generation of flights will be tethered launches. Eventually, future launch vehicles for LEO will be retrofitted to be way beyond Aerostats for max efficiency for multiple CubeSats at a time. 

Tethered Launches

We want to utilize best in class Aerostats that are meant to hold in hydrogen or helium for prolonged periods of time (4 to 5 years in air and sky deployments) and 5000ft elevation launches.

Future Launch Vehicles

Stark Drones wants to have multiple variations of future launch vehicles from SPUAV to modular space planes that are sustainable and meant to go beyond LEO for future deployment and launch needs.
