FAQs (Some Market Comparisons)

16

What is different between Starkcom and Project Loon?  We have different design, architecture and telemetry choices all together. The main similarity with Google's Project Loon is that they use Aerostats  (as we use custom Helikites)  to deploy. We also treat our balloons with Ultra/Super High Float and use coating to protect the electronics.

17

What CubeSat deployment mechanism does Project Loon use as opposed to Starkcom? Project Loon is more focused on swarm communication at the LEO level, we are at this time more focused on stationary points and lower elevation. 

18

What transmission and architecture method do you use as opposed to Project Loon? We utilize WANET + Direct to Terminal Gateway, Loon however utilizes strictly specialized Electro-optical transmission. 

19

How do you compare to Starlink? We utilize Radiosonic communication and standard software defined networking, as well as a low power transceiver module focused on local frequencies over global. Starlink has intelligence tracking similar to ORBCOMM, higher Ku-band range, and utilizes optical multiplexing. We too integrate rerouting and multiplexing for signaling. 

20

Do you plan on integrating intelligence tracking and higher transmission capabilities in the near future? In the near future, we would utilize GPS and timestamping for intelligence tracking through path tracking, and can utilize high power, longer range components. For the low elevation stationary design, there is no need to do that. 

FAQs (Delivery and Fault Tolerance)

21

Are balloons the only available delivery mechanism?  We aim to use  balloons because the easier delivery method for low elevation, and the allowance for less signals to bounce. However, if there are helium shortages, black swan events, technical or delivery problems, backup mechanisms are a feasibility. The main technical breakthrough isn't balloons but rather signal computing and data compression for broader connectivity options.

22

What happens if balloons can't work? We can create high frequency antennae that can work as range extenders and setup a backup server for data clustering. This will allow for a fault tolerant software defined network. The goal is to accomplish both, but if one thing doesn't work over the other, the ultimate task needing to be accomplished is direct to terminal gateway and localized broad range connectivity.

23

How do you monitor SLA (Service Level Agreements) or uptime?  We monitor SLA directly though our dashboard. Our goal is to utilize the Starkcom Portal, Balena Cloud and Chirpstack for monitoring, with Starkcom Portal tackling devices, data, and usage, Chirpstack tackling devices and protocols, and Balena tackling device identifiers.

24

Can you tackle unlicensed 5G Spectrum? We aim to implement the unlicensed 5G Spectrum standard in the beginning along with hybrid mesh capabilities to tackle 5G in underserved/underdeveloped areas. The IP routing and standards will most likely directly utilize embedded 5G firmware packages.

25

Is WLAN or 802.11 your primary usecase for this technology?  No. We are offering standard connectivity in the beginning because this is the end user's need in the market, but we believe that accessing a 5G spectrum along with LoRaWAN and MQTT Messaging is the biggest need. Industrial IoT has the opportunity for double digit growth in Southeast Asia and Africa, especially with all the water scarcity resources, the greater need for drones and sensors, and the underserved opportunities for industrialized farming. Smart Agriculture and Smart Farming is what Starkcom Global believes will put Africa in the world stage, along with other underrepresented communities.
